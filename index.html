Dear Rosita,

If we could do this life over
I'd give you the bigger universe.
Let you have the room
With more sunlight, with the bookshelf
That won't fall apart in less than 2 years.

Hand you every working umbrella,
Pour you every glass of milk,
Turn the year of white flowers
Over to you when
It falls into my hands
Instead of letting it pull me out of the ground.

If we could do this over,
I'd ask your parents to give you the softer spine,
Not as a sign of vulnerability
But as an indication of comfort.

I like to imagine a lot of things,
When you were six
You could bend over backwards
And touch the backs of your knees.

Now I'm the one standing slack-jawed in spring,
Spinning in circles under a paper moon
That I send you photos of
On the nights where we do not meet.

But all I want to know is
Was today one of the okay days?
Did you eat breakfast?
Did you take a nap in your cozy little bed
That despite its tiny appearance,
Seems capable of holding the world?
Is it alright if i hold your hand for a few more seconds?

You tell me everything about yourself
And yet,
You tell me nothing about yourself,
But I hope, that someday
You'll breathe easy without
All the clockwork ticking inside your tiny
But beautiful and surprisingly hilarious brain.

In my dreams,
I pull every good thing out of my chest
And press them into your palms
And you blink at me, confused.

"Take them", I say.
I've had my share of blessings
And my share of youth.

Someone told you that
All the white flowers were dead
But I saved one just for you.
It's going to grow into a very tall tree,
And the tree is going to
Eat all the monsters under the bed.

In my dreams I rearrange the past
So that you are the one who emerges
First into the shining light-of-the-world,
Dazed with all this destiny,

And then I hug you, very tightly, and whisper:
"Take my body and run."

When the time comes,
I hope you do :)

Love always,
Batman
